Companies want you to hack them. Here's where.

Not theory, not CTFs. Real platforms paying real money to ethical hackers
who find real bugs.

HackerOne — The largest and most established platform in the industry. Over
$300M paid out since 2012. Hosts programs from Uber, Spotify, GitHub, Slack
and the U.S. Department of Defense. Top hunters earn $250K+ per single bug.

Bugcrowd — Founded the same year as HackerOne. Used by OpenAI, Atlassian,
Mastercard and T-Mobile for bug bounties and managed pentests. Strong
enterprise integration with Jira and ServiceNow.

Intigriti — Europe's leading platform, based in Belgium. Backed by Microsoft,
Intel, Red Bull, Nestle and Ubisoft. Best entry point for European hunters
and known for the most hacker friendly UX in the industry.

Synack — Invite-only with around 1,500 vetted Synack Red Team members across
80+ countries. Hybrid hourly plus bounty model. Targets enterprise and
government clients including the IRS, Allianz and Navy Federal Credit Union.

YesWeHack — French platform with strong presence in Europe and Asia. Hosts
programs from OVHcloud, Doctolib, Tencent and ZTE. GDPR compliant
infrastructure and the platform of choice for many EU public sector programs.

Immunefi — The dominant Web3 platform, paying out the largest bounties in
cybersecurity history. Awarded a $10M payout for the Wormhole bug, the
highest ever paid. Trusted by Chainlink, Arbitrum, MakerDAO and most top
DeFi protocols.

HackenProof — Web3 bounty platform from the Hacken security group. $22M+
paid to hackers, 70K+ researchers. Hosts programs for Gate.io, NEAR,
MetaMask, Aptos and Polygon. More accessible alternative to Immunefi.
