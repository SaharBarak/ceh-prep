**Date:** 2026-05-20 (UTC)
**Target:** `http://localhost:3000` — `main` branch, dev build
**Methodology:** Playwright captured 3 viewport screenshots (hero / mid-scroll / footer @ 1440×900). 3 parallel agents (general-purpose, multimodal) — each given one ICP persona — viewed all three PNGs via native vision, reacted in-voice, then ran 3-pass chain-of-critique (re-read → missed → aggregate). Synthesis (this layer) merged convergent signals and called out persona-specific divergences.

## Convergent friction (cited by ≥2 personas)

### 1. The lab is told, not shown — **3/3 personas, unanimous**

> Sarah: *"They reference WebVM in the hero, mention it in a content card, but I never see a screenshot of the actual lab interface. For a product whose differentiator is 'hands-on lab in your browser,' not showing the lab is a real miss."*
> Alex: *"No screenshot of the actual WebVM / terminal. They TELL me there's a browser-tab lab, but they don't SHOW it."*
> Priya: *"The 'open the live terminal' link is the right move but it's a click away. For someone deciding whether to start the 3-day trial, an embedded interactive shell on the landing page would close the deal."*

**Severity: highest.** The browser-based WebVM is the page's stated differentiator from Udemy/EC-Council courseware; it's mentioned in copy 3× and shown visually 0× in the screenshots the personas saw.

> ⚠️ **Methodology note.** A mock terminal section *does* exist in the source (`app/src/app/page.tsx:152-264` — section 3, "It opens. You type. It grades you."). It did NOT appear in any of the three screenshots captured at hero / `totalHeight/2` / footer. Two interpretations:
> - **The capture missed it:** the section sits in dead viewport space between the hero and `totalHeight/2`. → Future runs should add a 4th screenshot at e.g. `totalHeight * 0.35` or capture full-page.
> - **The section is real but not load-bearing:** if 3 well-instructed multimodal agents looking at the page didn't surface "I saw a mock terminal" in their notes, it's either not visually prominent enough to land or not in the natural scroll path. Either way the user signal is the same: **the lab is invisible to a first-time visitor.**

### 2. Refund clause is the strongest line and it's buried — **3/3, with calibration disagreement**

All three personas independently said the refund-tied-to-simulator-score sentence is the most honest pricing copy they've seen in the category — and all three said it lives 3+ scrolls deep.

> Alex: *"The most honest cert-prep copy I've seen. Specific, falsifiable, accountable."*
> Sarah: *"The strongest single line on the page and it's the last thing I read."*
> Priya: *"A confident move. They tied the refund to a number from the in-product simulator. That tells me they actually believe in the simulator's predictive validity."*

⚠️ **Three agents extracted three different numbers** from the same page (Sarah: 65%, Alex: 60%, Priya: 80%). The actual copy (`page.tsx:389`) is **80%**. That the number is hard to read consistently is itself a signal — the refund condition is the most important number on the page and it's small-text in a corner. **The page is currently leaving conversion on the table by hiding its strongest line.**

### 3. No named human anywhere on the page — **2/3 (Sarah loud, Alex moderate; Priya silent)**

> Sarah: *"Zero named humans on the page. Not one face, not one quote, not one 'Maria Chen, passed on April 12.' In a market that's 95% cert mills, the absence of a single proof-of-outcome human is the loudest gap."*
> Alex: *"No named human / no 'built by' credential. 'People who actually use nmap' is anonymous. Drop one founder name with a real cert credential (OSCP, OSWE, CEH itself) and a LinkedIn — credibility doubles."*

Priya **actively distrusts testimonials** ("for my persona this is fine — I distrust testimonials"). So the bar is **one real human with a real credential**, not "5 testimonial cards." A founder signature with an OSCP/OSWE/CEH credential and LinkedIn closes Sarah and Alex without offending Priya's allergy.

### 4. Pricing & "no card required" not visible in hero — **2/3 (Alex loud, Sarah implicit; Priya: not friction)**

> Alex: *"Pricing buried at the bottom. For a money-anxious buyer, surfacing '$30/mo · cancel anytime' directly under the hero CTA removes the 'am I being upsold' fear before I even start scrolling. No 'no credit card required' anywhere visible. This is the single biggest missing reassurance for a 3-day-free trial."*
> Sarah: *"I want to see the menu before I sit down."*

Priya explicitly didn't care about pricing surfacing — $30 is invisible spend for the engineer persona. But for **the lower half of the income distribution within the ICP** (Sarah, Alex), this is the difference between clicking and bouncing to compare with free YouTube playlists.

## Divergent friction (persona-specific, single-source)

### Sarah — wants social proof
Sarah is the only persona who **needs a face on the page**. Alex would accept a founder credential; Priya would prefer none. Sarah is the persona for whom one named, photographed person who passed CEH is the single highest-leverage addition.

### Alex — wants the trial trap reassurance
Alex burned by free trials that auto-bill. **"No credit card required"** under the trial CTA is the single phrase between his finger and the button.

### Priya — wants advanced-track signal + v13 AI module
Priya is the only persona deep enough to catch:
1. **Hero tool list (nmap/sqlmap/hashcat) is the CEH floor, not ceiling.** Missing: impacket, BloodHound, responder, burp. → Signals the bar might be at CEH, not above it.
2. **CEH v13 specifically added AI/ML attack content as a domain.** She doesn't see "AI-augmented attacks" or "prompt injection / LLM attacks" anywhere in the visible curriculum. *"For a v13-marketed course in 2026, that's a gap."* → Either it's folded under another module (label it) or it's missing (add it).
3. **"80% of pass mark" refund is weaker than it sounds:** *"80% of 70% is 56% raw — a low bar to refund-disqualify. Pin a real number ('if you don't hit 70%, refund')."*

## Recommended actions (ranked by expected conversion impact)

### Priority 1 — show the lab
The unanimous top friction. Three options in increasing order of build cost:

a. **Cheapest:** add a static screenshot of `drill check ✓ 5/6 correct` output (the exact mock-terminal content from `page.tsx:230`) directly into the hero, replacing one of the stat cells in the receipts panel — or as a small inset below the hero subhead.
b. **Medium:** move the existing mock-terminal section (`page.tsx:152-264`) higher so it shows in the natural mid-scroll viewport. Currently sits in dead space.
c. **Gold standard:** embed a real interactive WebVM mini-shell on the marketing page. Priya: *"I'd convert in the terminal, not on the marketing page."* This single change has the highest expected lift for the technical persona.

### Priority 2 — surface refund + price + trial reassurance under the hero CTA
One line under "Start free — 3 days":
```
$30/mo  ·  cancel anytime  ·  no card required  ·  80%-of-CEH-v13-pass-mark or full refund
```
This single line addresses **friction items 2, 3, 4, and 5** simultaneously and was the most-requested ask across the 3 persona reviews. Lowest-cost, highest-coverage intervention.

### Priority 3 — add one named human + one credential
A single signature element somewhere visible — footer is fine. Pattern:
```
Built by Sahar Barak · OSCP/CEH/[whatever cert applies] · [LinkedIn]
```
Closes Sarah's friction. Adds credibility for Alex. Does not bother Priya (one founder name is not "5 testimonial cards"). The credential is load-bearing — a name without a relevant cert is weaker than the current anonymous "people who use nmap."

### Priority 4 — Priya-specific, but cheap: signal an advanced track
Add a kicker in the curriculum grid or between curriculum and closing CTA:
```
Plus an advanced lane — HexStrike-AI workflows, BloodHound walkthroughs,
advanced sqlmap tampers. For HTB / TryHackMe regulars.
```
Currently the only signal of advanced content is in the bonus blog cards, which Sarah read as testimonials (misframed). Promoting this to a top-line landing element converts the engineer-tier persona at near-zero cost.

### Priority 5 — verify CEH v13 AI-attack module
Priya is right: v13 added AI/ML attack content as a domain. The visible curriculum doesn't label it. Either:
- It's covered under another module — **label it explicitly** ("Day 12: AI-augmented attacks & defense") so Priya stops thinking the curriculum is stale, or
- It's not covered — that's a real curriculum gap and a v13-specific liability.

This is a content question, not a copy question. Resolve in the curriculum source (`app/src/lib/content/`).

### Optional: rename or repurpose "Field-notes from people doing the work"
Sarah misread this section as testimonials and was disappointed. Either:
- Rename to *"From the lab notebook"* / *"What we've been writing"* / *"Bonus library"* (already exists as `/bonus`), or
- Actually put practitioner notes there — short, first-person, signed.

## Methodology notes & follow-ups for the harness

1. **Capture coverage gap.** The `totalHeight / 2` mid-scroll screenshot landed on the bonus cards section, not the mock terminal. **All 3 agents independently surfaced "the lab is told not shown"** despite the mock terminal existing in the source. Add a 4th screenshot at `totalHeight * 0.35` (or switch to full-page captures + crop) so the harness doesn't artificially produce this finding.
2. **Number-extraction drift.** Three agents read three different refund thresholds from the same screenshot (65/60/80 — actual: 80). For numbers that are load-bearing, **the QA harness should cross-reference against repo source**, not trust OCR-style extraction from screenshots.
3. **Verdict scale could be wider.** All 3 personas landed on `would_consider` — the page is plausibly *better* than the rubric can detect. Consider extending the verdict scale to 5 levels (e.g. `would_bail / would_lurk / would_consider / would_trial / would_pay_now`) or adding a 0-10 conversion-confidence score so future reviews can detect smaller deltas.
4. **Persona pool.** This run used 3 of the 5 ICPs (Sarah, Alex, Priya). Marcus (SOC L1, time-constrained) and Dave (IT manager, team buyer) were skipped this run. Dave specifically would have surfaced "no team/seats option" — a different category of friction not covered here. Rotate or alternate the 3-of-5 pool across nightly runs.
